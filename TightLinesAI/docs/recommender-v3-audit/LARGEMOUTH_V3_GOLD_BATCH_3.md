# Largemouth V3 Gold Batch 3

## Purpose

Batch 3 is the first largemouth batch built after the daily nudge and color logic were explicitly locked down.

Its job is not to test architecture. That part is already in place.
Its job is to pressure-test whether the sharpened V3 logic can hold up across:

- more lake/pond windows than river windows
- a balanced mix of `clear`, `stained`, and `dirty` water
- high-value largemouth months that still matter nationally

## Batch 3 Focus

Batch 3 emphasizes:

- largemouth as a lake/pond-first species
- clarity discipline instead of another stained-heavy batch
- target-fishing versus horizontal-search separation
- variety driven by daily conditions inside believable monthly lanes
- stronger critique of whether top 1 is truly the best opener, not just “acceptable”

## Batch 3 Shape

- `10` scenarios total
- `8` lake/pond
- `2` river
- clarity mix:
  - `4` clear
  - `3` stained
  - `3` dirty

## Scenario List

1. `lmb_florida_apr_spawn_lake_clear`
   Why: clear tropical spawn discipline and target-fishing sharpness
2. `lmb_florida_aug_grass_lake_heat_dirty`
   Why: dirty late-summer Florida grass and bold cover-oriented control
3. `lmb_texas_jan_reservoir_cold_stained`
   Why: stained midwinter south-central restraint
4. `lmb_texas_jul_reservoir_brush_dirty`
   Why: dirty summer reservoir ledge/brush realism
5. `lmb_georgia_dec_highland_clear`
   Why: clear early-winter highland baitfish discipline
6. `lmb_louisiana_mar_grass_lake_prespawn_dirty`
   Why: dirty southern prespawn with grass and visible baitfish/craw lanes
7. `lmb_ozarks_may_postspawn_clear`
   Why: clean postspawn shad-following behavior
8. `lmb_minnesota_sep_weed_lake_stained`
   Why: northern stained early-fall weedline decisions
9. `lmb_alabama_jul_river_stained`
   Why: midsummer river largemouth shade/current behavior without lake blur
10. `lmb_california_mar_delta_clear`
   Why: clearer prespawn Delta behavior and cleaner current-cover separation

## What Batch 3 Must Tell Us

1. Does the explicit daily nudge logic still produce strong largemouth leads?
2. Are clear and dirty water recommendations now as confident as stained water recommendations?
3. Does the engine maintain proper lake/pond emphasis for largemouth?
4. Are river largemouth still clearly distinct from lake largemouth?
5. Is the engine giving real variety based on daily conditions without becoming volatile?

## Review Rubric

Score every scenario on a `0–2` scale:

- `2` = clearly right
- `1` = acceptable but not ideal
- `0` = wrong or concerning

Required categories:

1. `seasonal_fit`
2. `daily_fit`
3. `water_column_fit`
4. `archetype_fit`
5. `color_fit`
6. `top3_variety`
7. `boundedness`

## Failure Clusters To Track

- `seasonal_lane_wrong`
- `daily_overreaction`
- `daily_underreaction`
- `clarity_logic_wrong`
- `lake_river_blur`
- `grass_lane_wrong`
- `cold_clear_color_wrong`
- `dirty_water_visibility_wrong`
- `top3_too_redundant`
- `missing_classic`

## Why Batch 3 Matters

Batch 1 proved the model.
Batch 2 showed the engine could be tuned sharply.
Batch 3 is the first batch built to validate that the sharper V3 logic can scale across a more balanced largemouth spread without sliding back into ambiguity.
