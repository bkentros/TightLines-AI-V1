# Trout V3 Focus

Trout is the third freshwater V3 tuning species after largemouth and smallmouth.

Why trout is next:
- it forces the engine to stay river-only and disciplined
- it pressure-tests streamer-first fly logic more than any other freshwater species
- it should feel clearly different from bass and pike
- seasonal restraint matters more, especially in warm-tailwater summer and winter control windows

## Tuning Priorities

Trout should feel strongest in:
- freestone and tailwater rivers
- clear to lightly stained water
- streamer and minnow windows
- sculpin, leech, bugger, and baitfish movement

Trout should not collapse into:
- generic bass baitfish recommendations
- too much surface noise outside the small windows where mouse or popper logic is truly justified
- overly warmwater power-fishing behavior

## Primary Critique Questions

For each trout scenario, we should ask:
- Does this feel like a strong streamer-minded trout recommendation?
- Is the river reading disciplined enough?
- Are sculpin, bugger, leech, minnow, and articulated baitfish lanes showing when they should?
- Are tailwater summer scenarios tightening appropriately instead of getting too aggressive?
- Do colors stay realistic for trout streamers and minnow-style lures?

## Water-Type Emphasis

Trout V3 is:
- `freshwater_river` only

There is no lake / pond audit path for trout in this phase.

## Acceptance Bar Before Moving On

Trout is ready to freeze when:
- gold batches are consistently strong
- trout reads stay clearly different from bass
- fly recommendations feel especially trustworthy
- warm-tailwater summer and cold winter behavior stay bounded and biologically sensible
