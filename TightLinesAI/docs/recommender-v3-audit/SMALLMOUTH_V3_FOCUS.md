# Smallmouth V3 Focus

Smallmouth is the second freshwater V3 tuning species after largemouth.

Why smallmouth is next:
- it is strategically important
- it strongly pressure-tests river logic
- it needs clearer separation from largemouth behavior
- it uses several highly species-specific classics like `tube_jig`, `ned_rig`, `hair_jig`, and `inline_spinner`

## Tuning Priorities

Smallmouth should feel strongest in:
- rivers
- clear to lightly stained lakes
- cooler seasonal windows
- current seams, rock, baitfish, and crawfish transitions

Smallmouth should not collapse into:
- largemouth-style grass/cover recommendations
- too much flipping/frog logic
- generic bass behavior that ignores current and rock

## Primary Critique Questions

For each SMB scenario, we should ask:
- Does this feel like a strong smallmouth recommendation and not just a generic bass recommendation?
- Does the water type matter enough?
- Are river current, seam, and rock lanes being respected?
- Are tubes, Ned rigs, hair jigs, jerkbaits, inline spinners, and baitfish streamers showing up when they should?
- Are colors aligned with common smallmouth reality in clear, stained, and colder conditions?

## Water-Type Emphasis

Smallmouth should be tuned across both:
- `freshwater_river`
- `freshwater_lake_pond`

Unlike largemouth, SMB should not be lake-heavy by default.
The tuning program should treat river and lake as equally important.

## Acceptance Bar Before Moving On

Smallmouth is ready to freeze when:
- gold batches are consistently strong
- the engine clearly separates SMB from LMB
- river recommendations feel confident and species-correct
- top-3 outputs show real smallmouth variety without drifting into nonsense
